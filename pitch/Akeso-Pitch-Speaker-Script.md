# Akeso Pitch — Speaker Script

Target length: approximately 4½ minutes. The English below matches the speaker notes embedded in the revised PowerPoint.

## How to use this file

Present in English. The Chinese version is a faithful meaning-and-delivery reference for rehearsal; do not read both languages aloud during the pitch.

Keep this Markdown open beside the PowerPoint. Each `Slide N` heading matches the same numbered slide in the deck: read the English paragraph, use the Chinese paragraph only when you need to confirm the meaning, and follow the delivery cue at the end.

## Slide 1 — Reclaim Your Usable Time

I believe most teams here have built an app, a website, or a tool to help people save time. Akeso does that too. But our bigger goal is to give people back time they already have, yet cannot use.

A free hour is not useful if you have no energy, focus, or emotional capacity to act. Akeso is an explainable energy and nutrition coach that turns plans into action.

**中文对应稿：**

我相信在场大多数团队都在做一个 App、网站或工具，帮助人们节省时间。Akeso 也能做到这一点，但我们更大的目标，是把人们本来拥有、却无法真正使用的时间还给他们。

如果一个人没有精力、专注力或情绪容量去行动，再多一个空闲小时也没有意义。Akeso 是一个可解释的能量与营养教练，把计划转化为真正能够执行的行动。

中文提示：开场停顿一下，重读 “time they already have, yet cannot use”。

## Slide 2 — Your Energy Is Trying to Tell You Something

These memes exaggerate two extremes: your body feels like an alarm, or suddenly everything feels easy.

We are not claiming to measure cortisol. The serious question underneath the joke is this: why can the same person have a plan on both days, but only have the energy to execute it on one of them?

**中文对应稿：**

这两张 meme 把两种极端状态夸张化了：一种是身体像在拉警报，另一种是突然觉得一切都很轻松。

我们并不声称自己能测量皮质醇。玩笑背后的严肃问题是：为什么同一个人在两天都有计划，却只有其中一天有精力把它执行出来？

中文提示：先让观众看图和笑一下，再迅速把话题拉回 execution。

## Slide 3 — The Execution Gap

Most people do not have a planning problem. They already know what they want to do. The hard part is execution.

Imagine a student with five hours blocked out for work. Those five hours exist on the calendar. But after five hours of sleep, poor nutrition, dehydration, or a stressful morning, how many of those hours are genuinely usable?

We cannot add hours to the clock. We can work on the gap between time available and capacity to act.

**中文对应稿：**

大多数人的问题并不是不会做计划。他们其实知道自己想做什么，最难的是执行。

想象一位学生给自己安排了五小时工作。这五小时确实在日历上；但如果昨晚只睡了五小时、饮食不足、缺水，或者早上压力很大，其中到底有多少小时是真正可用的？

我们不能给一天增加更多钟点，但我们可以解决“拥有的时间”和“有能力行动的时间”之间的差距。

中文提示：指向左侧 “5:00”，然后指向右侧问号。

## Slide 4 — Tired Doesn’t Mean Lazy

That is why tired does not mean lazy. Low energy is the visible outcome, but the signals around it are scattered.

Food, sleep, hydration, stress, and mood can all shape the day someone experiences. Most productivity tools only see the task, so they treat every hour as identical. Akeso starts with the person who has to perform the task.

**中文对应稿：**

所以，累并不等于懒。低能量只是我们看见的结果，背后的信号却是分散的。

饮食、睡眠、补水、压力和情绪都会影响一个人当天的状态。大多数效率工具只看任务，因此把每个小时都当成一样；Akeso 从真正需要完成任务的人开始理解。

中文提示：最后一句是本页重点，放慢速度。

## Slide 5 — A Personal Energy and Nutrition Coach

Our solution is a personal energy and nutrition coach.

In the MVP, Akeso connects a short daily check-in with an explainable energy picture, an energy-aware plan, and food guidance grounded in what the user actually has.

It does not ask people to push harder. It helps them choose work, meals, and recovery that fit the day they actually have.

**中文对应稿：**

我们的解决方案，是一个个人能量与营养教练。

在 MVP 中，Akeso 将简短的每日签到、可解释的能量图、基于能量的日程，以及结合用户现有食材的饮食建议连接起来。

它不是要求用户更拼命，而是帮助他们选择适合当天真实状态的工作、饮食和恢复方式。

中文提示：指向右边手机画面，让评委看到这是已经完成的产品。

## Slide 6 — How It Works

The experience has three steps.

First, the user checks in with a few signals.

Second, they calibrate the result. This is not a mysterious black-box score: the user’s reported energy anchors the deterministic score. If the result does not reflect how they feel, they update the check-in and Akeso recalculates it.

Third, Akeso turns that state into a realistic next action—such as moving demanding work to a stronger window, or suggesting a meal from ingredients already available.

**中文对应稿：**

整个体验只有三步。

第一，用户用几个简单信号完成签到。

第二，用户校准结果。这不是一个神秘的黑箱分数：用户自己报告的能量状态是确定性评分的核心。如果结果不符合用户感受，他可以更新签到内容，Akeso 会重新计算。

第三，Akeso 将状态转化为一个现实的下一步，例如把高难度工作放到精力更好的时间段，或根据现有食材推荐一餐。

中文提示：三个步骤分别指向三台手机；不要说成用户可以任意修改分数。

## Slide 7 — Personalised, Not Medicalised

Personalised does not mean medicalised.

Akeso does not diagnose iron deficiency from a meal log. It can identify a repeated pattern, explain it cautiously, suggest one realistic food option, and recommend professional advice when symptoms persist.

The goal is useful guidance without pretending to be a doctor.

**中文对应稿：**

个性化不等于医疗化。

Akeso 不会仅凭饮食记录就诊断用户缺铁。它可以识别重复出现的模式，谨慎地解释这个模式，给出一个现实的饮食选项，并在症状持续时建议用户咨询专业人士。

我们的目标是提供有用的建议，而不是假装自己是医生。

中文提示：强调 safety 是产品设计的一部分，不是免责声明。

## Slide 8 — Measurable Time Saved

Akeso gives time back in two ways.

First, it removes daily decision friction. In our measured demo workflow, checking the calendar, ranking tasks, deciding what to eat, and building a plan took twelve minutes. Akeso reached the first useful recommendation in twenty-five seconds: eleven minutes and thirty-five seconds saved.

That is one rehearsed workflow, not a population study, so wider user testing is our next validation step.

Second, better timing helps people make more of the hours they already have.

**中文对应稿：**

Akeso 通过两种方式把时间还给用户。

第一，它减少每天做小决定的摩擦。在我们测量过的 Demo 流程中，查看日历、排序任务、决定吃什么、再安排日程需要十二分钟。Akeso 在二十五秒内就能给出第一条有用建议，也就是每天节省十一分三十五秒。

这是一次经过排练的 Demo 流程，不是大规模人群研究；因此下一步我们会做更广泛的用户验证。

第二，更好的时间安排能帮助人们更充分地使用他们本来拥有的时间。

中文提示：数据要说得坚定，但主动交代这是 demo workflow，显得可信。

## Slide 9 — Built to Explain, Not to Pretend

Under the hood, the Expo app talks to an Express API backed by Supabase.

Deterministic TypeScript services calculate the authoritative energy result and plan. AI can explain schema-validated guidance, but it does not invent the score.

That separation is important: the same input produces a consistent result, and uncertainty is communicated instead of hidden.

**中文对应稿：**

在技术上，Expo App 通过 Express API 与 Supabase 连接。确定性的 TypeScript 服务负责计算权威的能量结果和计划。

AI 可以解释经过 schema 验证的建议，但不会凭空生成能量分数。这种分离很重要：相同输入会得到一致结果，而不确定性会被清楚表达，而不是被隐藏。

中文提示：技术部分控制在半分钟内，重点解释为什么架构提高可信度。

## Slide 10 — Understand Your Energy. Feed It Better.

Today, Akeso connects daily energy, planning, and nutrition. Next, we want to deepen the picture with an emotion coach, faster food logging, wearables, weekly patterns, and expert-reviewed guidance.

Most tools save a few minutes. Akeso aims to do something deeper: help people reclaim the hours they are currently unable to use.

We do not ask people to grind harder. We help them understand their energy and use it at the right time.

Akeso: less grind, better timing.

**中文对应稿：**

今天，Akeso 已经把每日能量、计划和营养连接起来。下一步，我们希望通过情绪教练、更快的食物记录、可穿戴设备、每周模式和专家审核建议，让这张图更完整。

大多数工具只能节省几分钟。Akeso 想做得更深：帮助人们找回那些因为状态不好而无法使用的时间。

我们不要求人们更拼命。我们帮助人们理解自己的能量，并在正确的时间使用它。

Akeso：少一点硬撑，更好的节奏。
